# BhartX Academy — Product Principles

These principles form the foundation of BhartX Academy. Every feature, line of code, and design layout must align with these values.

---

## 1. Learning Over Watching
Passive consumption does not lead to understanding. We replace binge-watching with Active Recalls, Mid-Video prompts, and immediate Quiz exercises to engage the brain actively.

## 2. Mastery Before Progression
A student cannot advance to consecutive lessons until they show a minimum of 80% understanding in concept checkpoints. Progress represents comprehension, not simply scrolling or video progress.

## 3. Active Recall Before Revision
Students must explain concepts in their own words (Feynman Method) and challenge themselves before reading prepared summaries. Active cognitive retrieval is the primary driver of long-term memory.

## 4. Reduce Cognitive Load
Clean, distraction-free typography and minimal interfaces prioritize screen real estate for learning materials. We avoid cluttering layout widgets and animations.

## 5. AI Guides Before It Answers
Our AI systems act as Socratic mentors. The Tutor, Coach, and Examiner agents challenge student assumptions and deliver scaffolding hints instead of immediate copy-paste solutions.

## 6. Metacognitive Calibration
We track student confidence vs correctness to detect misconceptions (High confidence, wrong answer) and address low-confidence knowledge, training students to become self-aware learners.

## 7. Spaced Repetition Scheduling
We space study sessions at intervals (1, 3, 7, 15, and 30 days) to match the human forgetting curve, ensuring students review topics before memory strength decays.

## 8. Deliberate Practice
The Mistake Journal logs and prioritizes incorrect responses, directing student attention directly to areas of weakness rather than repetitive easy topics.

## 9. Blazing Fast Usability
A responsive, high-performance interface with optimized lazy loading maintains learner momentum. Simplicity beats feature bloat.

## 10. Every Decision Improves Learning Outcomes
If a design choice or new feature does not directly help students understand, retain, or apply knowledge, we do not build it.
